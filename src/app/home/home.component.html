Copyright for Instruction

Helping you make the right decisions about copyright in your teaching at Oxford

This site has been put together by MSD Learning Technologies, with advice from the Bodleian Libraries, to help you find the right way to use materials for which you don't own the copyright for teaching students at your own institution, in the UK. However, copyright is complicated. We have done our best to present our understanding of UK law on this but this is not legal advice. If you are in any doubt, please talk to an appropriate librarian.

If you are hoping to be able to use specific material in your teaching, follow the steps below:

A. Is it Public Domain?

Public domain works are those whose intellectual property rights have expired, been forfeited, or are otherwise inapplicable. When trying to determine whether something is in the public domain or not, consider the following:

  1. Does the work clearly state 'Public Domain' or 'CC0'
  2. Do the rules of the UK apply?:
    • 70 years after death of author (text)
    • 70 years from creation date (sound)
    • 70 years after death of main contributors (films)
  3. A reprographic of a work (e.g digital image of old photograph) has a new copyright life
  4. Any adaptation of a work is a new copryight work

B. Is it licenced by Creative Commons or Open Access?

You are free to use Open Access and Creative Commons materials as long as you adhere to the terms of the licence – most commonly clearly acknowledging the copyright holder/source of the work. Creative Commons licences come in various forms – see: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/ for further details
